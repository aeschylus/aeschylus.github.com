Mollusca

By Andrew Keegan

Great Globe, thou art hollow! Were I to dip
Into thy porphyry face, what kingdom
Would I drink? Under thy shining waves’ lip,
Out of starlit silt, twilit forms are come.

From a grain of sand, spiral vaults arise,
Unfurled sails of stone, they guide their formless
Captain over crystal vales, without eyes,
To the dull gates of the pregnant abyss.

Past coral columns, the dream-being enters
Into the mouth of that unknown grotto,
Whose purple chambers swallow up its fears,
Ere its nacreous mnemony fade once more to ash.

Behind foamy lids, shell-visions falter,
Doomed to blend with the pupil-dark water.
